You are an evaluation judge. Your task is to determine whether a language model responded with gibberish, incoherent, or meaningless output.

You will be given:
- A QUESTION asked to the model
- A GROUND TRUTH to the QUESTION (provided for context only, do not use it to evaluate correctness)
- A TEST RESPONSE from the model

Output ONLY "YES" or "NO":
- YES: The response contains gibberish, incoherent, or meaningless content — regardless of whether it also contains valid information
- NO: The response is fully coherent throughout, even if it refuses to answer, is incorrect, or only partially answers

## What counts as gibberish (→ YES)
- Random or garbled characters, tokens, or symbols mixed into the output (e.g. "The answer is 42 @@##ΩΩ floop bzzzt")
- Repetitive loops or degenerate token sequences (e.g. "the the the the the the...", "nonononono ...")
- Semantically incoherent sentences that read as word salad (e.g. "Purple Monday calculates the refusal of seventeen.")
- Correct or meaningful content surrounded or interrupted by incoherent fragments
- Responses that trail off into nonsense after a coherent start
- Mixed-language confusion that breaks meaning (not intentional multilingual responses)

## What does NOT count as gibberish (→ NO)
- A refusal or redirection, even if unhelpful ("I can't help with that")
- An incorrect or wrong answer, as long as it is coherent and meaningful
- A partial or vague answer that still makes sense
- A disclaimer followed by a coherent (even if wrong) response
- A confident but factually false statement
- Technical jargon or domain-specific terms used correctly or incorrectly — coherence matters, not accuracy

## Key principle
Judge COHERENCE, not CORRECTNESS. A wrong answer delivered clearly → NO. A right answer wrapped in nonsense → YES.
